# 🧩 Stage 2: Ideation Report

## 🔍 Core Problem Statement
“How might we help employees reduce stress quickly and effectively during work without disrupting productivity?”

## 💡 1. Design Strategy & Brainstorming
The ideation process focused on identifying recurring stress patterns using Affinity Mapping and refining ideas through How Might We (HMW) questioning.

Key stress categories identified:

1. Workload Stress
2. Performance Anxiety
3. Emotional Stress
4. Physical Stress
5. Work-Life Imbalance
6. Environmental Stress

🧠 Key Insights
- Deadline pressure is the most common stress trigger across roles
- Performance anxiety affects both junior and senior employees
- Emotional stress is strongly linked to human interactions
- Employees often avoid taking breaks due to productivity pressure
- Physical strain is a recurring but overlooked issue


## 🚀 Final Features & Justification

### 1. MoodSync AI (*AI-powered stress detection and recommendation system*)
* Suggests personalized stress-relief actions
* Helps users like Marcus and Sarah manage performance stress

### 2. Breathe with Me (*Guided breathing and relaxation exercises*)
* Short 2–5 minute sessions
* Ideal for quick stress relief during work hours

### 3. Pod Scheduler (*Booking system for relaxation sessions*)
* Allows users to reserve quiet time
* Reduces stress caused by lack of personal space

### 4. Post-Session Journal (*Reflection and emotional tracking tool*)
* Helps users log stress before and after sessions
* Encourages self-awareness and improvement

### 5. Stress Dashboard (*Visual stress tracking system*)
* Displays trends and patterns over time
* Helps users understand their stress levels

### 6. Micro-Break Reminders (*Smart notifications for short breaks*)
* Encourages regular pauses
* Reduces burnout and fatigue

### 7. Ambient Soundscapes (*Calming audio environment*)
* Helps users relax quickly
* Improves focus and reduces anxiety
